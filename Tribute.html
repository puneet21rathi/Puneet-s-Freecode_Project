Sundar Pichai

The tech visionary who shaped the digital age.

Here's a time line of Sundar Pichai's life:

  • 1972 - Born in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, India
  • 1993 - He earns a Bachelor of Technology degree in Metallurgical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur.
  • 1995 - He completes his Master of Science degree in Material Sciences and Engineering from Stanford University.
  • 1997 - He begins his professional career by working in engineering and product management positions at Applied Materials and McKinsey & Company.
  • 2004 - He joins Google in April 2004, initially working on the development of the Google Toolbar. He later leads the product management and innovation efforts for various Google products, including Google Chrome, Chrome OS, and Google Drive. He also gets married to Anjali Pichai in 2004.
  • 2008 - He oversees the launch of the Chrome web browser, which becomes a significant success and eventually dominates the browser market.
  • 2012 - He is promoted to Senior Vice President of Chrome and Apps, expanding his responsibilities to include overseeing Google's suite of applications.
  • 2013 - He gains further recognition for his role in Google's acquisition of Android Inc. in 2005, which led to the development of the Android operating system. He becomes the head of Android, in addition to his responsibilities with Chrome and Apps.
  • 2015 - In August 2015, he is appointed CEO of Google, taking over the reins from Larry Page, who became CEO of Alphabet Inc., Google's parent company.
  • 2016 - Under his leadership, Google restructures its various interests under the umbrella of Alphabet Inc., with Google becoming a subsidiary. He continues to lead Google as its CEO.
  • 2017 - He was a speaker at the World Internet Conference in China, where he stated that "a lot of work Google does is to help Chinese companies. There are many small and medium-sized businesses in China who take advantage of Google to get their products to many other countries outside of China."
  • 2018 - He was questioned by the United States House Judiciary Committee on a range of Google-related issues such as possible political bias on Google's platforms, the company's alleged plans for a "censored search app" in China, and its privacy practices.
  • 2019 - He becomes the CEO of Alphabet Inc., succeeding Larry Page and Sergey Brin, who stepped down from their roles. As CEO of Alphabet, he retains his position as CEO of Google as well.
  • 2020 - the United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation unanimously voted in favor of, and on a bipartisan basis, to subpoena Pichai, along with Facebook (today Meta) CEO Mark Zuckerberg and then Twitter CEO Jack Dorsey, to testify before the committee in response to lawmakers' concerns over the technology industry's exemption from Section 230 of the Communications Decency Act of 1934.
  • 2022 - He received the Padma Bhushan in the category of Trade and Industry[54] from the Government of India, the country's third-highest civilian award.
